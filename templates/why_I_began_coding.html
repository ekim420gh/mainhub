Why I began coding...

well, I had no idea. I wanted to try something new, I guess.

I mean don't we all? lol

I've been getting into defi as of late. The idea of decentralization is imagination provoking. Avalance and Cardano and Ethereum. I like their technologies and Bitcoin started it.

I'm amazed how far I've come as a programmer. I could barely print out "Hello World" the first time. Now I'm committing on the daily on GitHub and writing source code for Bloomberg.

For the next 3 months I'll be taking classes/lectures and working on assigned projects to increase knowledge/skills. I'm excited.

I'm proud of the progress. Slow but steady. Coding is creating. And creating feels good because we're putting life into something. Where's your energy going?

The more I learn, the more high I feel on top of shoulders of giants.

I started because I was curious and curiosity doesn't have an end.

I also coded for the money but.. wow, money doesn't fix your problems. That's a level of rich to realize that, no? I have enough even though I want to accumulate much much more.

I was greedy sometimes, but eventually doing this becamse my time of solitude. It was just me clacking on a keyboard searching for answers on stack overflow. Sharing knowledge is wealth too.

I believe building applications is meaningful.

Anyway, why ekim420?
